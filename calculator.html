About BolusCalc

BolusCalc helps you calculate insulin bolus doses based on your carbohydrate intake, current blood glucose, and personal insulin parameters. All data is stored locally on your device -- nothing is sent to any server.

Choose Your Units

Which unit does your glucose meter use?

Carb Ratios

Your insulin-to-carb ratio (ICR) tells you how many grams of carbohydrate one unit of insulin covers. For example, a ratio of 10 means 1 unit covers 10g of carbs. Your diabetes team can provide this number.

Use multiple ratios if yours varies by meal — e.g. 10 for breakfast, 12 for lunch. The calculator will show a dose for each.

Correction Factor

Your insulin sensitivity factor (ISF) is how much one unit of insulin lowers your blood glucose. For example, an ISF of 2.5 means 1 unit drops your BG by 2.5. Your diabetes team can advise.

Target Glucose

This is the blood glucose level you aim for. The calculator uses it to work out correction doses when your BG is above target.

Active Insulin Time

Duration of Insulin Action (DIA) is how long your rapid insulin stays active. Most rapid-acting insulins are active for 3-5 hours. Your diabetes team can advise.
